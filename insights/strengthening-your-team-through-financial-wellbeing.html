How proportionate financial wellbeing support strengthens engagement, retention and performance across SME and mid-sized teams.

Workplaces are changing, and so are the pressures placed on people. Rising household costs, complex pensions, and a growing focus on wellbeing mean that employees are looking for more support, clarity, and direction. At the same time, business leaders are under pressure to retain talent, improve performance, and build a culture where people feel valued.

To help address this need, Finch Theory plans to launch Finch Theory in the new year, a service designed to strengthen organisations through smarter benefits, financial wellbeing, and practical, everyday support.

Finch Theory provides a simple and effective solution.

  • Attract and retain the best talent
  • Boost productivity and engagement
  • Strengthen company culture
  • Reduce HR and operational pressures
  • Improve employee happiness, financial stability and security

What the Service Delivers

Finch Theory brings together three core elements that work together to improve confidence, engagement, and productivity across your team:

  1. Reviewing employee incentivesWe help you understand what is working, what is not, and where small changes can make a big difference.
  2. Employee benefits consultancyIndependent, clear guidance that helps you save money, strengthen your benefits package, and provide support that your team will actually use.
  3. Financial wellbeing workshops and one to one supportPractical, friendly sessions that help employees make better decisions about money, tax, pensions, and planning for the future.

These services are supported by Finch Theory, our financial planning arm that works with leadership teams and employees across the UK. Together, we offer a fully integrated experience for your organisation and your people.

Why businesses are choosing Finch Theory

Our approach is simple. We give your business a fresh pair of eyes, help you reduce unnecessary costs, improve staff engagement, and provide clarity for your people at a time when they need it most.

Employers who invest in financial wellbeing report stronger retention, reduced stress, and a more focused workforce. With budgets under pressure and recruitment costs rising, supporting your team has never been more important.

A new standard for workplace support

Finch Theory has been built in collaboration with trusted partners across wellbeing, incentives, health, marketing, and leadership development. This creates a holistic service that goes far beyond traditional employee benefits and brings meaningful support into everyday working life.
